Media Showcase: About Michael Jackson

Michael Jackson (1958–2009) was an iconic American singer, songwriter, and dancer
universally celebrated as the "King of Pop". As one of the most influential figures in
entertainment history, he revolutionized the music industry, broke racial barriers, and
popularized legendary dance moves like the moonwalk.

Musical Legacy

The Jackson 5: Michael began his career as the charismatic child prodigy and lead
singer of the family group, which signed with Motown in 1969 and scored hits like
"ABC" and "I Want You Back".

Death and Posthumous Presence

On June 25, 2009, Michael Jackson passed away at age 50 due to cardiac arrest caused
by a lethal combination of sedatives and propofol administered by his personal physician.
His death was ruled a homicide, and the doctor was convicted of involuntary
manslaughter.

Decades after his passing, his massive catalog of music continues to top charts
worldwide, and his influence remains a fixture in global pop culture, celebrated by
tributes like the Broadway hit MJ The Musical.
